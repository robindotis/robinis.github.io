The work archives

16 August, 2023

Today I did some physical archiving, as opposed to the virtual archiving I do here.

Colleagues of mine had spent the last few weeks disappearing into our archives, to clear them out ahead of our move to a new building.

Well it turns out most of them can be binned, so I spent Wednesday afternoon helping them finish off the process of sorting them into paper for recycling, CDs, plastic for the main rubbish and piles and piles of folders presumably for shipping into the past for reuse in a time when they were useful. All our archives have now been digital for years.

So it was really a case of chucking stuff out rather than archiving, but it felt good and I felt productive once I saw the boxes we filled! üòÄ

It was also interesting to see colleagues' names on the project folders from 15 years ago. I now feel slightly less frustrated when I have to ask them for the third time how to do things. I've only been in the unit 4 years!

Inaccessible Code

16 August, 2023

I like this summary from Eric of the types of people who create inaccessible code:

  1. People who create inaccessible code, but do not realize they are doing so.
  2. People who create inaccessible code and realize they are doing so, but do not know how to fix it.
  3. People who create inaccessible code, and do not care about fixing it.
  4. People who create inaccessible code that they think is accessible.
  5. People who create inaccessible code that our industry thinks is accessible, but experientially is not.

Surface Detail

27 July, 2023

by Iain M Banks

I got through this book in 10 days whilst on holiday. Reading it over such a short time frame helped me to keep the storyline fresh in mind. Maybe because of this, I feel it is certainly one of the better Culture series books. Somewhat unusually it ties up all the loose ends and leaves nothing unexplained. Maybe because by now readers are aware of the all encompassing power of the Culture civilisation and the trickery this enables them engage in. And yet it is not necessarily the most powerful civilisation.
